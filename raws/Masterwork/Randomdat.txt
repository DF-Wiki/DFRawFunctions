//Today, class, we're going to show you how scripts are made.
//The [BRACKETED] items are what are going to be added. The number directly to the right of the ; is the chance (from 1 to 1000)
//that they'll be added to a particular creature. Anything to the right of a ; following that is unused by the program.
//When you see a series of ?'s, such as the [CAN_LEARN] tags below, the RandCreatures will only select one of those, and the !
//tag above it. For example, a creature might get [INTELLIGENT], [CAN_LEARN][SLOW_LEARNER], or just [CAN_LEARN], but it will
//not get any combination of the three.
//For the "pref" "adj" "desc" "verb" and "noun" tags tabbed out underneath the entries, these relate to prefstrings and creature
//descriptions. A typical description is: "A [adj], [adj] [noun] with [desc] and [desc]. It [verb] and [verb]. An example:
//A [large], [scythed] [predator] with [a lack of eyes] and [spikes instead of legs]. It [is dangerous] and [very predatorial].
//Putting more than desc/adj/etc. entry on a single line, separated by :'s, will have the program choose ONE of the selection 
//to add to the list of possibilities. Including an X as an entry, as for [CARNIVORE] below, for example, will give it a chance
//to not do anything at all. Putting a + symbol in front of the word, like under [CARNIVORE] will make it choose only one
//of the plussed entries - thus, a [CARNIVORE] could have either noun:carnivore or adj:predatory, but not both. It also has a 50%
//chance of getting adj:predatory, a 50% chance of getting adj:scavenging, and a 100% chance of adding pref:eating habits.
//NOTE: This does NOT mean that they are guaranteed to get these tags. These tags are simply added to the lists and picked at
//random, which means you may or may not see them on a creature.
